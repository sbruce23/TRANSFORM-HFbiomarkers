Introduction

Brief Description: This document contains the results from the weighted linear and weighted logistic regressions and the CART analysis.

We want to test for changes in log2 Pro-BNP readings of patients from their baseline to the their 90 day follow-up visits. We do this with multiple linear regression models, where we utilize our calculated IPWs from our predicted and explanatory models and compare these findings with unweighted MLR models. We also present a weighted logistic regression model that tests whether the predicted log2 Pro-BNP at 90 day follow-up is less than 1000.

Finally, we performed a CART analysis using the function causalTree() from the htetree package.

Linear (Weighted) Regression Models

Biomarker Specific Models

In these models, we keep the baseline NT-ProBNP as an x variable with the same response variable of the 90-day change in NT-ProBNP from baseline to followup visits, but we introduce the other biomarkers alone to the lm() 90-day models. Moreover, we keep all the race, sex, and age variables for all of these submodels. So, in total we have 7 models with the following structure:

Response: 90-day log2 NT-ProBNP change

Covariates: race, sex, age, and NT-ProBNP baseline reading

Model-specific covariates: baseline reading of a biomarker other than NT-ProBNP and the interaction of that biomarker with the baseline reading of NT-ProBNP

Interpretating the Tree

In the log2 scaling, leaf nodes are interpreted as:

\[ [log2[FollowUp\_Tors] - log2[Baseline\_Tors]] - [log2[FollowUp\_Fur] - log2[Baseline\_Fur]] \]

In this scaling, a negative value means Torsemide is better and a positive value means Furosemide is better at reducing log2 NT-proBNP pg/ml from baseline to followup.

For undone log2 Transform, leaf nodes are interpreted as:

\[ \frac{\frac{FollowUp\_Tors}{Baseline\_Tors}}{\frac{FollowUp\_Fur}{Baseline\_Fur}} \]

In this scaling, a value less than 1 means Torsemide is better and a value greater than 1 means Furosemide is better at reducing log2 NT-proBNP pg/ml from baseline to followup.

The plots are shown in the undone log2 Transform unless specified.
